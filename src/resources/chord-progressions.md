# Chord Progressions Reference

## Universal Progressions (work in any key)

| Name | Degrees | Character |
|------|---------|-----------|
| Pop | I – V – vi – IV | Bright, universal |
| Blues | I – IV – I – V – IV – I | Earthy, bluesy |
| Jazz ii-V-I | ii7 – V7 – Imaj7 | Smooth resolution |
| 50s / Doo-wop | I – vi – IV – V | Nostalgic |
| Andalusian Cadence | i – VII – VI – V | Flamenco, dramatic |
| Pachelbel Canon | I – V – vi – iii – IV – I – IV – V | Elegant |

## Progressions by Mood

### Happy / Uplifting
```
I – IV – V – I        (e.g., C – F – G – C)
I – V – IV – I
```

### Sad / Melancholic
```
i – VI – III – VII    (natural minor)
i – iv – i – V        (classic minor)
```

### Mysterious / Tense
```
i – bVII – bVI – bVII
i – bII – V – i       (Neapolitan)
```

### Epic / Cinematic
```
i – VI – III – VII
I – bVII – bVI – V
```

### Jazz / Sophisticated
```
Imaj7 – vi7 – ii7 – V7
ii7 – V7 – Imaj7 – VImaj7
iii7 – VI7 – ii7 – V7   (turnaround)
```

## Common Chord Substitutions

| Original | Substitute | Reason |
|----------|------------|--------|
| V7 | bII7 | Tritone substitution (same tritone) |
| I | iii | Tonic substitution (shares 2 notes) |
| I | vi | Relative minor substitution |
| IV | ii | Subdominant substitution |
| V | vii° | Dominant function replacement |

## Modulation Strategies

- **Pivot chord** — use a chord common to both keys as a bridge
- **Direct modulation** — jump to the new key without preparation (effective for drama)
- **Chromatic mediant** — move to a key a 3rd away (e.g., C major → E major)
- **Circle of 5ths** — move through keys by 5ths (C→G→D→A...)

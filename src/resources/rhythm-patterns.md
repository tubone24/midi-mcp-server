# Rhythm Patterns Reference

## Time Signatures

| Signature | Feel | Common Use |
|-----------|------|------------|
| 4/4 | 4 beats, quarter note pulse | Pop, rock, jazz, classical |
| 3/4 | 3 beats (waltz) | Waltz, minuet, ballad |
| 6/8 | 2 compound beats of 3 | Compound duple; jig, barcarolle |
| 5/4 | Asymmetric; 3+2 or 2+3 | Prog rock, jazz (Take Five) |
| 7/8 | Asymmetric; 2+2+3 or 3+2+2 | Balkan, prog |
| 12/8 | 4 compound beats of 3 | Slow blues, gospel |

## Syncopation Techniques

| Technique | Description | Effect |
|-----------|-------------|--------|
| Backbeat | Accent on beats 2 and 4 | Rock, pop drive |
| Offbeat / Upbeat | Accent between main beats | Reggae, ska |
| Anticipation | Hit the beat 1/8th or 1/16th early | Jazz, funk, pop |
| Hemiola | 3-beat pattern over 2-beat framework | Rhythmic tension |

## Genre-Specific Groove Profiles

### Rock (4/4, 110–140 BPM)
- Kick: beats 1, 3; Snare: beats 2, 4
- Hi-hat: eighth notes throughout
- Bass follows kick drum closely

### Jazz Swing (4/4, 120–200 BPM)
- Hi-hat: beats 2 & 4 (laid back)
- Ride cymbal: jazz swing pattern (8th notes with long-short feel)
- Snare: ghosted 16ths, accents on 2 & 4
- Bass: walking quarter notes

### Funk (4/4, 90–115 BPM)
- Emphasis on beat 1 (the "One")
- Syncopated 16th-note grid
- Velocity variation is critical (ghost notes at ~40 velocity, accents at 110+)

### Bossa Nova (4/4, 120–140 BPM)
- Guitar pattern: | x . x . x . x . | (offbeat clave)
- Bass: root on beat 1, then syncopated
- Soft, laid-back feel; no strong backbeat

### Reggae (4/4, 65–90 BPM)
- Skank (upbeat chords): beats 2½ and 4½
- Bass: heavy, often on 1 and 3 with syncopation
- Kick: sparse, often on beat 3

### Electronic / House (4/4, 120–130 BPM)
- Kick: every quarter note (four-on-the-floor)
- Hi-hat: 8th or 16th notes; open hat on offbeat
- Synth bass: often sidechain-compressed to kick

## Velocity Guidelines

- **pp (pianissimo)**: 20–40
- **p (piano)**: 40–60
- **mp (mezzo-piano)**: 60–80
- **mf (mezzo-forte)**: 80–100
- **f (forte)**: 100–115
- **ff (fortissimo)**: 115–127

**Pro tip**: Vary velocity by ±10–15 per note to humanize patterns. Never use uniform velocity for all notes in a groove.
